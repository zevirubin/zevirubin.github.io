About Me

I am a Computer Science Major interested in the nexus of cutting edge electronics and mechanical devices. I have been building multirotors on my own and in makerspaces for the last four years and am excited to see how this technology can make the world a better place.

Passion

I started building drones as a hobby. The satisfaction of designing something capable of flight and taking it from the drawing board to the air is unbelievable. As my involvement in the hobby has grown I have seen the great potential it has for the world.

Education

I started my Computer Science BA in 2011 and will be graduating in Spring 2016. At Wheaton College I have been exposed to the well-rounded education a liberal arts school provides as well as having full access to an awesome Makerspace.

Videos

One of the early projects I started was a YouTube channel to document my projects and progress as a pilot. My work with multirotors led me to learn about aerial filming and mapping, which in turn, led me to develop a braod piloting skillset.

Experience

Data Visualization Research

I worked with the head of the Computer Science department over the summer on visualizing ranked, time-series data using graphic displays and a clustering display. The majority of the work was replicating a Flash Action Script version using Javascript and HTML5.

Tech Support Student Manager

I currently hold the senior most student position in the college tech support department. I have vast experience with basic computer functionality and trouble shooting as well as proficiency with Filemaker Pro, Deep Freeze, and bulk computer imaging.

Emergency Training Aerial Recon

I have had the opportunity to accompany the Bristol County technical rescue team to a number of training exercises and scenarios. Access to fast and accurate aerial inspection and recon was incredibly valuable to the response team.

Aerial Video

I have worked indevidually as well as with the Wheaton College communications department and Film and New Media studies department to help promote a positive view of drones and their capability through aerial footage and images, pannel talks, and flight training sessions.

Aerial Video

Since starting flying, one of the first things I did with my first Tricopter was put an old gopro on it. The unique viewpoint an aerial craft can give has always excited me and multirotors make it abundantly available,

My aerial experiance has mostly been limited to 2.7K, and more recently 4K GoPro video. However, I recently built myself my first big Aerial Photography rig that carries a Nex 5n camera on a custom 3-axis gimbal. The quality difference between action cameras and a mirrorless dslr is incredible. Both have their advantages and strengths.

My Aerial Videos and projects can be found on my YouTube account.
